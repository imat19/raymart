TAKI TACHIBANA

Taki is a high school teenager who must balance his work and social life while pursuing a future career in architecture. However, Taki’s life takes a dramatic twist as he finds himself inside Mistsuha Miyamizu’s body. After finding out their dilemma, Taki must work alongside Mitsuha to resolve their body switch problem.

Mitsuha Miyazumi

She is a female high school student who serves as her town’s priestess in her family shrine. At the same time, Mitsuha doesn’t enjoy her rural life because of her father’s stern desires and the criticism she receives as a shrine maiden. Mitsuha then states that she wants to be reincarnated in her next life as a boy and live in the city. Even though she achieved her goal by body-switching with Taki, she eventually learned about other issues that would spell doom for her and her entire town.

Yotsuha Miyamizu

Despite her young appearance, Yotsuha is an independent, energetic, and organized individual who wants to improve her knowledge about difficult topics in life. She is more dedicated to her duties than her sister, but she finds boredom in them too. Yotsuha always looks out for her sister, even if she thinks ill of her at times.

Katsuhiko Teshigawara

Katsuhiko Teshigawara is one of Mitsuha’s friends with expertise in machinery and explosives. Though his parents are never named, Katsuhiko worked under his father as a construction worker. Despite being a wiz at machinery, Katsuhiko had a crush on Mistsuha but wasn’t brave enough to share his feelings toward her.

Sayaka Natori

Sayaka is a teenage girl with average build and height. She has mid-length chestnut brown hair, which she wears in two braids pointing upwards and straight bangs that cover her forehead. She has light brown eyes and a mole under her left eye. After the timeskip, Sayaka cuts her hair to chin-length, which she keeps in a bobbed-hair style

Tsukasa Fujii

Tsukasa is a slim young man with an average build and height. He has dark blue eyes and black-blueish nape-length hair with free-falling bangs framing his face. He also sports transparent rectangular-shaped glasses. He was often depicted in his school uniform when he was a student.

As an adult, Tsukasa grows taller and develops sharper facial features. Rather than having free-falling bangs, he now slicks his bangs to the right, which gives a more professional appearance. He is also depicted in a black business suit.

Miki Okudera

Miki Okudera is one of Your Name’s side characters and works at an Italian restaurant with Taki Tachibana. At the beginning of the movie, Miki is Taki’s crush. Miki loves wearing beautiful clothes and boasting about her good looks. She has a girly personality. Despite her charm, Miki has a smoking addiction and finds it difficult to stop.

Hitoha Miyamizu

Hitoha is a calm and composed woman with a strong love for her family, especially her granddaughters. However, she isn't particularly fond of Toshiki since he rejected the Miyamizu shrine and its traditions to pursue a career in politics, calling him "that stupid son-in-law".

She is shown to be very traditional and takes her family's traditions very seriously, passing them down to her granddaughters. She initially objected to her daughter Futaba marrying Toshiki, an outsider; but she gave in when he agreed to marry into the family and enter priesthood

Shinta Takagi

Takagi is a tall young man with a large build. He has nape-length, slightly wavy light brown hair and free-falling bangs framing his face and parting in the middle. He also has thick eyebrows and dark brown eyes. He was usually depicted in his school uniform when he attended high school.

Toshiki Miyamizu

He is Mitsuha and Yotsuha’s father, who pursued politics as he didn’t want anything to do with his wife’s traditional lifestyle. He’s also a stern businessman and maintained his status as Itomori’s mayor for four years. While the film’s conclusion doesn’t discuss his whereabouts, he’s morally responsible for his town’s evacuation during the Comet Tiamat incident and has received criticism for his residents’ survival.
